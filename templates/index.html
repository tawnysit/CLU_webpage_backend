Caltech ZTF-II Census of the Local Universe

About the ZTF CLU Experiment:

The Zwicky Transient Faciility (ZTF) survey Census of the Local Universe (CLU) experiment is a volume and luminosity-limited supernova experiment.

In ZTF-I, the CLU experiment was a volume-limited experiment, where all sources were found at less than 200 Mpc (z<0.05) and no further than 100-arcsec from galaxies found the the Census of the Local Universe galaxy catalog.

The ZTF-II CLU experiment now is a volume-luminosity limited supernova sample. We attempt to save and classify all local (<150 Mpc / z<0.0331) SN that peak below an absolute magnitude of -17. We have also expanded the physical radius of the filter such that we try to save transients with a physical seperation less than 30 kpc from the CLU host galaxy (for z>0.01) and/or visible association with the host galaxy.
