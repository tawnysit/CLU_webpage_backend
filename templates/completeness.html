ZTF Census of the Local Universe - Completeness (2021)

The figure below plots the cumulative completeness for all saved CLU sources as a function of apparent magnitude. The sources used are filtered to be brighter than -10 in absolute magnitude (this removes some non-SN sources such as nearby classical novae). Because our largest classification telescope, Keck I/LRIS, can only really observe targets brighter than ~21 apparent magnitude, we also only consider sources that meet that apparent magnitude cut. For the ZTF-II CLU experiment as of approximately May 2021, we now only assign spectroscopic follow-up to local (<150 Mpc), subluminous (peak below an absolute magnitude of -17) sources no more than 30 kpc from a CLU galaxy (the yellow line).

The table below presents classification success statistics by limiting magnitude for subluminous sources within the CLU volume (<150 Mpc), no more than 30 kpc from a CLU galaxy, and meeting the previously described magnitude cuts. In other words, the last row (Total) corresponds to the subluminous, <150 Mpc, <30kpc cumulative completeness shown in the above plot by the yellow line.
